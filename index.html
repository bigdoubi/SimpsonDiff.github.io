Abstract

Differential driven robots are widely used in various scenarios, such as skid-steering and tracked robots. The differences in the driving mechanism require different kinematic models to describe them. What's more, considering the nonholonomic dynamics and possible lateral sliding, a comprehensive trajectory optimization framework is needed for efficiently computing trajectories for such robots. In this paper, we propose a universal trajectory optimization framework that can be applied to common differential driven robots, enabling the generation of high-quality trajectories within a limited computation time. We introduce a novel control quantities polynomial trajectory that adequately describes robots' motion. We formulate the trajectory optimization problem with lower complexity while ensuring safety and operational efficiency. We enhance the entire trajectory planning system to improve its robustness. The effectiveness of our algorithm has been validated through simulations and real-world experiments.

Experiments

Two-wheel differential driven robot

TRACER MINI is employed as our two-wheel differential driven robot platform, which is controlled through linear and angular velocity. Map is pre-built and the motion capture system is utilized for localization.

Skid-steering robot

SCOUT MINI is employed as our skid-steering robot platform, which is controlled via linear and angular velocity. We randomly place obstacles and use fastlio for localization, simulating the real-world application.
